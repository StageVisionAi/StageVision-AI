The Origin

It Started With
A Posing Coach

StageVision AI began as a personal problem — helping competitive physique athletes nail their stage presentation. The original system, Apex, was built to analyze human movement and posing in real time using a custom-built Apex Movement Engine (AME).

Apex never shipped. Because somewhere in building it, the real problem revealed itself: not posing — perception. Not coaching — intelligence. The pivot wasn't a decision. It was inevitable.

Today StageVision AI is an independent research lab in everything but name. No degree. No funding. No institution. Just a third shift assembly worker in Fort Wayne, Indiana, pulling on a thread that won't let go.

"It always does when you start pulling on a real problem."

What started as coaching logic for athletes became the foundation for a new class of vision-language model running on consumer hardware.

Where It Began

Apex &
The AME

The original StageVision AI product — never released, but never wasted. Apex was an AI coaching system built to analyze human movement, positioning, and control in real time. It ran on a custom-built proprietary system called the Apex Movement Engine.

Apex — AI Coaching System
Designed to evaluate posture, angles, symmetry, and stability to deliver clear, actionable feedback — just like a professional coach would. Built initially for competitive physique athletes on stage, the system was domain-agnostic by design. The same intelligence was being expanded into strength training, physical therapy, rehabilitation, and athletic movement analysis.
Patent Pending
Apex Movement Engine (AME)
The proprietary perception layer beneath Apex. The AME goes beyond static analysis — detecting pose quality and consistency, identifying inefficiencies and imbalance drift, providing real-time coaching cues, and locking optimal positions for deeper review. It brought real coaching logic into the digital age. The principles built into AME became the conceptual foundation for SoMoS.
Foundation of SoMoS

Apex isn't just an app feature — it's a scalable AI coaching platform that pivoted into something bigger. The pivot wasn't a failure. It was the work revealing what it actually wanted to be.
